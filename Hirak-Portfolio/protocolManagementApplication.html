D.E.P.M.A

Web Enabled Database Application


The aim of this project is the research, analysis, design and development of a Web-based database-driven research management system to support the exchanges of physical and digital materials within the scientific consortium with a view to better assist the conduct of experiments and thus to produce experiments of higher quality and ensure greater transparency and accountability.

This prototype was built for NANOSCIMAX- an european scientific research project in the field of nanomedicine, which aims to explore the possibility to develop a new generation of nanomaterial-driven imaging systems.It is a three year long project which is funded by the European Comission as part of Horizon 2020 research programme.It is conducted by a cross-institutional consortium involving a network of around fifteen scientific research sites located all over Europe. The sixty or so scientists who work in the labs at these sites and who are involved in this project need to conduct complex experimental work to evaluate how nanomaterials can be used as imaging markers to detect a number of health-related disorders. This experimental work is often distributed between sites as it involves a certain number of researchers from different labs conducting experiments cooperatively and thus often require very tight coordination. Every site stores various pieces of scientific apparatus and a range of scientific materials required to conduct the experiments. In order for these experiments to be rigorously conducted at different sites, the scientists often have to exchange samples of scientific materials which are typically stored on respective sites.

The DEPMA prototype had to fullfill the following objectives.

  • To identify new and existing groups of scientists and sort them in order of the experiments they are working in.
  • To define a clear framework of new and existing protocols which registered scientists can access.
  • To facilitate a flexible approach of posting materials to required sites.
  • To classify experiments and their status of completion with the functionality to upload and publish reports etc.

A Functional prototype was created which met the above requirements.The application was coded in PHP. This is a project for the module "Web Enabled Database Applications" on the Multimedia MSc(Interaction Design and Computing) course in University of Westminster.
